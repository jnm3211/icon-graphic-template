A proposal is going to reshape downtown Jamaica, Queens.

Some residents say it does not include their input.

The Jamaica Neighborhood Plan is an initiative to rezone a large portion of downtown Jamaica, Queens over the next 15 years to create thousands of new residential homes and commercial developments. The proposal is currently up for approval by the New York City Council, but residents and community stakeholders say they’re concerned the proposal does not take input from current Jamaican residents who would be impacted by the new developments.

Residents say the proposal could displace residents and local businesses. The high rises could overshadow small residental houses that also occupy the area. A portion of the neighborhood is in a flood plain and residents and community leaders are concerned the plan does not include provisions to address flooding concerns.
